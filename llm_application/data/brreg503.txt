



The general meeting in a house building cooperative

Last updated: 9 March 2022.





The house building cooperative must record minutes from the general meeting. 

The general meeting is the supreme governing body of the house building cooperative. The unit holders have the right to appear and vote here. The house building cooperative must keep an ordinary general meeting by the end of June each year. All other general meetings are extraordinary general meetings.
What disclosures should the minutes contain?
The minutes must at least contain information about

which house building cooperative is holding the general meeting
the date and the place of the meeting
how many unit holders are represented
the approval of the summoning of the meeting
decision made on the general meeting

The minutes must be signed by the chair of the meeting and at least one other person present.

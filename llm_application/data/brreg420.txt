



Who has accounting obligation?

Last updated: 28 February 2022.





It is the type of organisation, size of the business activity, or whether you are subject to supervision that determines whether you have accounting obligation. 









 
                                Here you will find an overview of who this applies to                            






All imited companies (AS) and public limited companies(ASA)
All housing cooperatives (BRL) andÂ house building cooperatives (BBL)
All foundations
All public corporations
All unit trusts
All jointly owned shipping companies
General partnerships (ANS/DA) and other participant-like companies that have

more than NOK 5 million in sales revenue
number of employees corresponding to an average of 5 man-years
more than 5 partners, or
partners which are legal persons with limited liability


Sole proprietorships which have

assets worth more than NOK 20 million, or
employees constituting to more than 20 man-years on average


Co-operatives and economic associations with sales revenue exceeding NOK 2 million
Other associations which during the year had assets worth more than NOK 20 million, or the number of employees exceeded more than 20 man-year on average
Condominium flat owners with 21 or more amount of sections
Norwegian registered foreign businesses practicing or participating in activity in Norway, or on the Norwegian continental shelf and are subject to Norwegian taxation in accordance with internal Norwegian legislation
Auditors, accountants and estate agents as well as other enterprises that are supervised by the Financial Supervisory Authority of Norway
Others which pursuant to special provisions, or according to law, have accounting obligation



When the accounting obligation depends on size limits, you will be required to report annual accounts when the limit has been passed two years in a row. For the obligation to cease, you will have to be below the limit two years in a row.




Enterprises with accountabe obligation, shall draw up annual accounts consisting of at least profit and loss accounts, balance sheet and notes.
Enterprises that according to the Accounting Act are small enterprises, have simplified accounting rules and do not need to draw up an annual report.
The accounting information is to be publicly available.
